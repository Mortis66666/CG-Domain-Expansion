  The Goal

The aim of this game is to expand your domain, the one with the larger domain wins.

  Rules

The game is played on a 7x7 board, where each cell is identified by coordinates ranging from (0, 0) in the top-left corner to (6, 6) in the bottom-right. Each player will control a token.

Movements

During a player's turn, the player may either keep their token in its current location, or move it to another cell up to 3 steps away (measured in Manhattan distance). There must be an unobstructed path (not blocked by any wall or the other player's token) to be able to move to the new location. A token cannot be moved outside the board, or to a cell occupied by the other player's token.

Wall building

After completing their move – or choosing not to move – the player must build a wall on any side of their token: Up, Down, Left, or Right. After a wall is built, neither player's token will be able to walk through it. A wall cannot be built on an existing wall, see "Expert Rules" section to learn what happens if you attempt to do so.

Game over condition

A game is over if the walls on the board splits both players to two completely separated domains, meaning that neither player can reach the cell occupied by the other player.
Victory Condition
You have a larger domain (more accessible cells) than your opponent.
Defeat Condition
  • Your program does not provide a command in the allotted time or it provides an unrecognized command.
  • You have smaller domain (fewer accessible cells) than your opponent.



  Expert Rules

If you attempt to reach a cell exceeding the 3-steps limit, you will move 0 steps instead.

If you attempt to build a wall on an existing wall, your wall building direction will be rotated clockwise until an available side is found.

  Game Input

Initial input

Line 1: 2 space-separated integers width height, the size of the board

Input

Line 1: 2 space-separated integers x y, the position of your token

Line 2: 2 space-separated integers opponentX opponentY, the position of your opponent's token

Line 3: An integer numWalls, number of walls

Next numWalls lines: 4 space-separated integers ax ay bx by, position of two cells, a and b, denoting a wall between the cells, a is always either above or to the left of b

Walls are given in chronological order
Output
A single line x y direction message, x and y denotes the position of the cell you wish to move your token to (or its current position if not moving), direction denotes the direction you wish to build your wall, message to print a message, optional.
direction is denoted by U, D, L or R
Constraints
Response time for first turn ≤ 1000ms
Response time for one turn ≤ 100ms
